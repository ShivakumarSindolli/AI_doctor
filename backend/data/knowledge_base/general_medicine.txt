GENERAL MEDICINE - COMMON SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS

FEVER
Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature often due to illness. Normal temperature is around 37 degrees Celsius or 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit. A fever is generally a temperature above 38 degrees Celsius or 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit. Causes include bacterial and viral infections, heat exhaustion, certain inflammatory conditions, and some medications. Management includes rest, adequate hydration, and fever-reducing medications like acetaminophen or ibuprofen. Seek medical care for fever above 39.4 degrees Celsius, fever lasting more than 3 days, or fever with severe symptoms such as stiff neck, confusion, difficulty breathing, rash, or severe headache.

HEADACHE TYPES
Tension headache is the most common type, causing a dull aching sensation all around the head with tightness around the forehead or the back of the head and neck. Migraine is a severe headache often with nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. It can last from hours to days. Cluster headaches are extremely painful headaches that occur in clusters. Warning signs requiring urgent care include sudden severe thunderclap headache, headache with stiff neck and fever, headache following head injury, and progressive headache worse over days.

DIABETES
Type 1 diabetes occurs when the immune system destroys insulin-producing cells. Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body does not produce enough insulin or does not use it effectively. Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, extreme hunger, unexplained weight loss, blurry vision, slow-healing sores, and frequent infections. Management includes blood sugar monitoring, dietary changes, exercise, and medications or insulin as prescribed by a doctor. Uncontrolled diabetes can lead to serious complications affecting kidneys, eyes, nerves, and cardiovascular system.

RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS
Upper respiratory infections affect the nose, throat, and sinuses. Common cold symptoms include runny nose, sore throat, cough, and mild fever. Influenza is more severe with sudden onset of fever, chills, muscle aches, headache, cough, and fatigue. Pneumonia affects the lungs and causes cough with phlegm, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. Seek urgent care for difficulty breathing, high persistent fever, blood in cough, chest pain with breathing, or symptoms that worsen after initial improvement.

PAIN ASSESSMENT
Pain location, character, severity, duration, and aggravating or relieving factors all provide important diagnostic information. Sudden severe pain requires urgent evaluation. Pain accompanied by fever, vomiting, neurological symptoms, or cardiovascular symptoms requires immediate medical attention. Chronic persistent pain warrants evaluation by a healthcare professional to determine the underlying cause.

DEHYDRATION
Dehydration occurs when you lose more fluid than you take in. Symptoms range from mild thirst and dark urine to severe symptoms including dizziness, rapid heartbeat, rapid breathing, sunken eyes, lack of urination, confusion, and fainting. Severe dehydration requires immediate medical treatment. Prevention involves adequate fluid intake especially during hot weather illness or physical activity.

DIABETES EMERGENCY - HYPOGLYCEMIA
Low blood sugar symptoms include shakiness, sweating, hunger, confusion, rapid heartbeat, pale skin, and weakness. If conscious give fast-acting sugar such as fruit juice regular soda glucose tablets or honey. If unconscious seek emergency medical care immediately.
