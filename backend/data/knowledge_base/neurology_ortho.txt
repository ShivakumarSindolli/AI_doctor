NEUROLOGY - BRAIN AND NERVOUS SYSTEM

STROKE - FAST RECOGNITION
Use the FAST acronym to recognize a stroke:
F - Face drooping: Does one side of the face droop or is it numb? Ask the person to smile.
A - Arm weakness: Is one arm weak or numb? Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
S - Speech difficulty: Is speech slurred? Is the person unable to speak or hard to understand?
T - Time to call emergency services immediately if you observe any of these signs.
Other stroke symptoms include sudden numbness or weakness in face arm or leg especially on one side of the body, sudden confusion or trouble understanding, sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes, sudden trouble walking dizziness or loss of balance, and sudden severe headache with no known cause.

EPILEPSY AND SEIZURES
A seizure is a sudden uncontrolled electrical disturbance in the brain. Seizure first aid includes: stay with the person, keep them safe from injury, do not restrain them, do not put anything in their mouth, time the seizure duration, place them on their side after convulsions stop, stay until they are fully conscious. Call emergency services if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes, the person does not regain consciousness, a second seizure follows immediately, the person is injured, the person is pregnant, or it is the person's first seizure.

MIGRAINE MANAGEMENT
Migraine is characterized by intense throbbing headache usually on one side of the head. Associated symptoms include nausea, vomiting, and extreme sensitivity to light and sound. Some migraines are preceded by aura including visual disturbances. Triggers include hormonal changes, foods and drinks, stress, sensory stimuli, sleep changes, physical activity, weather changes, and medications. Management includes identifying and avoiding triggers, rest in a quiet dark room, hydration, and medications prescribed by a neurologist.

ORTHOPEDICS - BONES JOINTS AND MUSCLES

BACK PAIN
Back pain is one of the most common medical problems. Most back pain gradually improves with home treatment and self-care within a few weeks. Causes include muscle or ligament strain, bulging or ruptured discs, arthritis, osteoporosis, and structural problems. Warning signs requiring urgent care include back pain after injury, back pain with weakness numbness or tingling in one or both legs, loss of bladder or bowel control, severe constant pain, pain with fever, unexplained weight loss with back pain. Conservative management includes rest for short periods, heat or cold application, over-the-counter pain relievers, and gradual return to activity. Prolonged bed rest is not recommended.

JOINT PAIN AND ARTHRITIS
Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis involving wear-and-tear damage to joint cartilage. Symptoms include pain during or after movement, joint tenderness, stiffness, loss of flexibility, and bone spurs. Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune condition causing joint inflammation. Symptoms include tender warm swollen joints, joint stiffness especially in the morning, fatigue, fever, and loss of appetite. Management includes physical therapy, pain medications, lifestyle modifications, and in some cases surgery. Consult a rheumatologist or orthopedic specialist.

SPRAINS AND STRAINS
RICE protocol for acute musculoskeletal injuries:
R - Rest: Avoid using the injured area
I - Ice: Apply ice wrapped in cloth for 20 minutes several times a day
C - Compression: Use an elastic bandage to wrap the injured area
E - Elevation: Keep the injured limb elevated above the level of the heart
Seek medical care if there is severe pain, inability to bear weight, significant swelling, numbness, or if symptoms do not improve within a few days.
