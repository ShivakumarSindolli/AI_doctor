CARDIOLOGY - HEART AND CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONS

HYPERTENSION (High Blood Pressure)
Hypertension is a condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high. Normal blood pressure is below 120/80 mmHg. High blood pressure is 130/80 mmHg or higher. Most people with high blood pressure have no symptoms, which is why it is called a silent killer. Risk factors include obesity, smoking, sedentary lifestyle, high salt intake, stress, family history, and age. Lifestyle modifications include reducing sodium intake, regular physical activity, limiting alcohol, quitting smoking, and maintaining a healthy weight. Medication may be prescribed by a cardiologist.

CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE
Coronary artery disease develops when the major blood vessels that supply the heart with blood oxygen and nutrients become damaged or diseased. Cholesterol-containing deposits called plaque in the coronary arteries are usually responsible for the condition. Symptoms include chest pain or angina, shortness of breath, and in severe cases heart attack. Symptoms of a heart attack include crushing chest pain, pain radiating to the left arm or jaw, sweating, nausea, and shortness of breath. Seek emergency care immediately if heart attack is suspected.

HEART FAILURE
Heart failure occurs when the heart muscle does not pump blood as well as it should. Conditions such as narrowed arteries or high blood pressure gradually leave the heart too weak or stiff to fill and pump efficiently. Symptoms include shortness of breath when lying down or with exertion, fatigue, swelling in legs ankles and feet, rapid or irregular heartbeat, reduced ability to exercise, and persistent cough. Management includes lifestyle changes, medications, and in some cases surgical procedures.

ARRHYTHMIAS
Arrhythmias are irregular heartbeats where the heart beats too fast, too slow, or irregularly. Common types include atrial fibrillation, bradycardia, tachycardia, and ventricular fibrillation. Symptoms include fluttering in the chest, racing heartbeat, slow heartbeat, chest pain, shortness of breath, lightheadedness, and fainting. Some arrhythmias are harmless while others can be life-threatening and require immediate medical attention.

CHEST PAIN - WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE
Seek emergency medical care immediately if you experience chest pain accompanied by: shortness of breath, sweating, nausea or vomiting, pain that spreads to the arm, back, neck or jaw, feeling of pressure squeezing fullness or crushing in the chest. Never ignore unexplained chest pain as it may indicate a heart attack.

CHOLESTEROL AND CARDIOVASCULAR RISK
Total cholesterol should ideally be below 200 mg/dL. LDL bad cholesterol should be below 100 mg/dL. HDL good cholesterol should be above 60 mg/dL. High cholesterol increases the risk of heart disease and stroke. Diet changes, exercise, and medications prescribed by a physician can help manage cholesterol levels.
