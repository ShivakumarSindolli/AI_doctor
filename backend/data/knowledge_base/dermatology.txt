DERMATOLOGY - COMMON SKIN CONDITIONS

ECZEMA (Atopic Dermatitis)
Eczema is a chronic inflammatory skin condition characterized by dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. It commonly appears in childhood but can persist into adulthood. Typical symptoms include intense itching especially at night, dry and sensitive skin, red to brownish-gray patches, small raised bumps which may weep fluid, thickened or cracked skin. Common triggers include dry skin, stress, sweat, soaps and detergents, dust mites, pollen, and certain foods. Management includes regular moisturizing, avoiding triggers, topical corticosteroids prescribed by a doctor, and antihistamines for itching.

PSORIASIS
Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid buildup of skin cells, resulting in scaling on the skin surface. Scales typically develop on joints like elbows and knees, though the condition can affect any body part. Symptoms include red patches covered with thick silvery scales, dry cracked skin that may bleed, itching burning or soreness, thickened pitted or ridged nails, and swollen stiff joints. Psoriasis triggers include infections, stress, smoking, heavy alcohol consumption, certain medications, and skin injury. Treatment options include topical creams, light therapy, and systemic medications prescribed by a dermatologist.

ACNE VULGARIS
Acne is a skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells. It causes whiteheads, blackheads, and pimples. Acne appears most often on the face, forehead, chest, upper back and shoulders. Types include comedonal acne, inflammatory acne, cystic acne, and hormonal acne. Treatment depends on severity and includes cleansers, topical retinoids, benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, and in severe cases oral medications prescribed by a physician. Avoid squeezing or picking acne lesions.

CONTACT DERMATITIS
Contact dermatitis is a red itchy rash caused by direct contact with a substance that irritates the skin or triggers an allergic reaction. The rash is not contagious but can be very uncomfortable. Common causes include soaps, cosmetics, fragrances, jewelry containing nickel, plants such as poison ivy, cleaning products, and latex. Treatment includes identifying and avoiding the irritant, applying cool wet compresses, anti-itch creams, and in severe cases topical steroids prescribed by a doctor.

FUNGAL INFECTIONS
Common fungal skin infections include tinea pedis (athlete's foot), tinea corporis (ringworm), tinea cruris (jock itch), and candidiasis. Ringworm causes a ring-shaped red scaly patch. Athlete's foot causes scaling, flaking, and itching between toes. These infections are treated with antifungal creams available over the counter, or prescription antifungals for severe cases. Keep affected areas clean and dry.

SKIN CANCER WARNING SIGNS (ABCDE Rule)
A - Asymmetry: One half unlike the other half
B - Border: Irregular ragged notched or blurred border
C - Color: Variation of color from one area to another
D - Diameter: While melanomas are usually larger than 6mm, they can be smaller
E - Evolving: A mole or skin lesion that looks different from the rest or is changing in size shape or color
Any suspicious lesion should be evaluated by a dermatologist immediately.
